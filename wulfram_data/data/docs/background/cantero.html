Eventually, when I get it written for real, there will be a story of a battle here. It is a concrete illustration, or sampling, of the point in time at which society mutated into the game's "present day" situation, and helps explain why there are a bunch of small groups of people with tanks fighting over patches of land.

There was this thing called the Coalition Government, which through the inertia of time had become this big agency that controlled pretty much all of the civilized worlds that humanity had inhabited (though most of the galaxy was still unexplored).

The Coalition Government was not excessively martial or imperialistic, and neither was it too big-brotherish, though like all modern governments, it had slight tendencies toward all three. They were very concerned about budget, and they cared very much about avoiding messy headlines.

There was this planet called Cantero, and on this planet were a very large number of members of the Independent Party who claimed that the local elections for planetary governors had been fixed (in those elections, the Independent Party had lost very very badly). The situation became very tense when local Independent Party groups on Cantero began building their own large-scale munitions.

The Coalition Government "quarantined" the offending Independent Party areas (which were the size of small towns) and cut off their means of supply. They were building tanks and other offensive units, but all visual evidence showed that it would take them months to manufacture enough to be any real threat, and they would not last that long.

The Coalition took a very defensive stance: they surrounded their important governmental centers with heavy defenses that would easily shred any oncoming tanks. The occasional minor attack would come, and they would defeat it. They never attacked the Independents, for fear of bad publicity (the stories of "burning bodies of innocent civilians" would be inevitable), and economics (when assaulting the enemy's bases, they'd lose equipment and suffer casualties. If they just wait, the enemy would collapse).

The Coalition didn't know about Portals, though, because they hadn't been invented yet. And about one week before their resources ran out, it became clear why the Independents hadn't built very many tanks: inside big warehouses they'd been cranking out a number of portals. They performed a tactic that became known as a "portal rush": leave four or five portals half-set-up (one end placed, the other end still in a box) and get one tank through defenses into an enemy area, long enough to drop a portal. Then you have a gate inside, and the second guy flies through and drops his other portal. Repeat until you run out of portals; meanwhile your other forces are flying through and smacking the energy units.

Using this tactic, for which the Coalition had been completely unprepared, the Independents took control of all the planet's governmental centers within three days.
