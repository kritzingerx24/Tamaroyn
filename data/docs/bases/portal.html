A portal is a doorway between two locations in space, formed by two devices that serve as endpoints.

Portals are often referred to as "the doors to independence" and "the passage to the modern day", referring to the Independent Party's landslide victory over the Coalition Government at the Battle of Cantero in 2642. Most serious historians, however, downplay the portal's role in the Revolutionary War, citing it as one specific example of the general technological progress that made the war's outcome inevitable.

At least one endpoint of a portal must be powered in order for friendly forces to pass through; and regardless of the state of the destination endpoint, if the source point is controlled by hostile forces, it will be impassable.

Because the two halves of a portal are connected by topological isocontours, when one half is destroyed, the other end will collapse and explode.
